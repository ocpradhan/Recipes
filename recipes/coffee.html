Coffee

Description

According to CraftCoffeeSpot, about 41 percent of Americans use a drip coffee maker, and about 27 percent own a single-cup system, with some overlap between the two camps. But, what about the java fans who don't own one, don't have access to power (say, on a camping trip), or prefer to make their coffee in a more classic way?

Enter these four ways to make coffee without a coffee maker such as with a French press or a pour-over coffee maker. However, if you don't have one and prefer not to invest in new equipment, these ways to make coffee should be doable with the items you already have around the house.

Ingredients

  • Six ounces of cold water
  • 1 tablespoon of coffee
  • Sugar
  • Cream

Steps

  1. Start with six ounces of cold water per serving. Then, pour water into a small saucepan over medium-high heat.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of coffee per serving to the pan. If desired, add sugar now.
  3. Bring the coffee mixture to a boil.
  4. Stir occasionally and allow it to boil for 2 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat, let it sit for 4 minutes, then pour the coffee slowly and carefully from the pan so the grounds stay in the pan â€” and out of your cup(s).
  6. Add sugar and/or cream, if desired.
